## [Corruption erodes social trust more in democracies than in autocracies](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/political-science/articles/10.3389/fpos.2026.1779810/full)
* Research indicates that corruption has a stronger negative impact on social trust in democracies compared to autocracies [https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/political-science/articles/10.3389/fpos.2026.1779810/full]
* The study uses country-level data to correlate perceived corruption and generalized trust, finding a strong association in democracies and a weak or absent one in autocracies [https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/political-science/articles/10.3389/fpos.2026.1779810/full]
* The research paper, titled "The Price of Accountability: Corruption Erodes Social Trust More in Democracies Than in Autocracies," is available on OSF [https://osf.io/a8m4r]
